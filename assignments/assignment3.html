Assignment 3 Visualization Critique & Redesign

Yishi Wang — yishi@mit.edu — 11.C85 Spring 2025

Insight & Critique

Insights:

  • Unique & Intuitive Design – The spiral timeline effectively represents time as a continuous cycle, similar to natural patterns like the Earth's rotation or moon phases.
  • Seasonal Patterns & Recurring Trends – The 12-month cycle clearly shows seasonal trends in COVID-19 cases, particularly highlighting the winter surges at the end of 2021 and early 2022.
  • Comparative Time Progression – The layered spiral format allows for easy comparison of different years (2020–2022) and encourages readers to consider potential trends in upcoming months.

Critique:

  • Lack of Clear Axis Labels & Scale – The curved axis lacks well-defined scales, making it difficult to interpret precise values. Readers may overestimate or underestimate the magnitude of different peaks.
  • Absence of Exact Data Points – While the visualization effectively shows trends, it lacks specific numerical markers, making it harder to extract precise insights.
  • Flat Color Scheme – The single-tone red shading does not provide strong contrast between different peaks, reducing the clarity of trend fluctuations.
  • Unnecessary Central Axis – The central radial axis does not add meaningful value and could be removed or redesigned to improve readability.

Final Visualization Design

Design Decisions

The radial design reflects natural cycles, like moon phases and the Earth's rotation, reinforcing the concept of time progression while facilitating direct yearly comparisons. Each ring represents a 12-month cycle, allowing for direct comparison of yearly trends in both new cases and death numbers.

The largest surges are represented by larger bubbles, high mortality numbers are indicated by warm-colored bubbles, with densely clustered event markers in the background. This design naturally draws attention to the peak winter months. After observing the broader trends, readers are encouraged to zoom in on specific periods by consulting the timeline captions.

This final visualization aims to strike an optimal balance between clarity, engagement, and data precision by addressing critiques from earlier iterations. Improvements include clear axis labeling, precise data references, the removal of the central point, and enhanced color dynamics. The style and color palette draw inspiration from Giorgia Lupi's "Data Humanism," creating an engaging yet data-driven narrative.

Final Reflection

  • Hierarchy, Structure and Visual Consistencyare essential when combining multiple layers of information into a single visualization. Hierarchy and Structure Helps guide the reader through the data and Visual Consistency creates a cohesive narrative.
  • Combining Broad Trends with Context Presenting broad trends alongside background context and detailed data points enhances the depth of the narrative and bolsters the credibility of the visualization.
